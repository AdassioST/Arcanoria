#character

[[Primary Instrument]]: [[Portharmonica]] (A hybrid device: part music box, part calculation engine, producing tones that correspond to divined futures)

[[Armament]]: [[Spindle-Staff]] (A staff topped with a spinning apparatus)

[[Soul Leitmotif]]: [[Luminance]] (Realization that magic can also be beautiful)
[[Ornament]]s: [[Resonance]] (Finding his authentic self through the fusion of invention and magic), [[Flux]] (The raw emotional truth of discovery and experimentation)

- *"I see clearly, and that clarity must mean something."

*Healthy Path: "I see clearly, but that doesn't mean I must act on everything I see. Some truths are burdens others don't deserve to carry."

*Anti-Hero Path: "I see the futures no one else can see. I will speak them all, and they will fear me, and fear will make them obey."

![[Adass_httpss.mj.runS4Ujn0LRlKI_steampunk_gnome_divination_wizar_076bb5ee-206b-4a8d-87ca-f34dd08f550f.png]]

A young inventor and divination wizard whose family raised him on systematic lies about magic's nature. His parents, merchants of the [[Sundered Vale]], built their security on a foundational myth: that magic was corruption, that [[Spellweaver]]s were sources of contagion, that any manifestation of the [[Soul Leitmotif]] was a mark of internal rot that would consume the family. They did not suppress [[Gayviene]]'s [[Luminance]] binding outright—they poisoned his perception of it. When his primary binding manifested, they taught him that what he perceived as clarity was delusion. What he saw as truth was dangerous obsession. The gift that should have liberated him became a source of profound shame.

So [[Gayviene]] learned to hide his visions, to question his own perceptions, to believe that his ability to see through deception was itself a form of deception. He became a creator of mechanisms instead—things made of metal and mathematics, where magic could not hide. His inventions were brilliant but soulless, machines that worked perfectly because they relied on nothing but their own logic. In the mechanisms, he found refuge: precision without magic, order without the corruption his family promised.

He was entirely alone.

Until a traveling wizard arrived.

The sage came to the city seeking shelter, and by chance (or destiny woven into [[The Eternal Symphony]]), [[Gayviene]] found her collapsed near his workshop. When he brought her inside, something in her recognized something in him. She was a [[Spellweaver]] of [[Strand]] and [[Crystal]], a user of old magic that was almost extinct in the region. Within days of recovering, she recognized the signs: a young man with raw [[Luminance]], unawakened and poisoned by those who should have honored it.

One evening, as they sat together, the wizard asked him a simple question: "What would you want to share your life with, if you could choose freely?"

[[Gayviene]] had no answer. He had never allowed himself to imagine such a thing.

The wizard taught him then—not through lecture, but through ritual. She showed him the words of summoning, the intention required to call forth a familiar from the [[Auric Heptacode]], a spirit-bond that would tie a creature of the material world to his own [[Consciousness]]. She told him that a familiar was not a servant or a tool, but a mirror of his own soul made manifest—a companion born from his deepest, most authentic self.

"Speak the words," she said. "And let what comes be what you truly need."

[[Gayviene]] spoke. His hands moved through the gestures, his voice shaped the syllables, and in the space between intention and manifestation, something extraordinary occurred. A small weasel—fierce and golden-eyed, trembling with the newness of consciousness—materialized in the ritual circle. Not summoned from elsewhere, but born directly from [[Gayviene]]'s [[Fundamental Frequency]], given form through the binding.

[[Scrap]] was born.

![[Adass_httpss.mj.runI8l4fkaWv-k_enchanted_weasel_familiar_copper_801b7253-2dc1-41ec-a58a-909f1e514319 1.png]]

The weasel was [[Gayviene]]—or rather, [[Gayviene]]'s authentic self given independent life. Small, fierce, loyal, and entirely free of the shame and poisoning his family had instilled. In that instant of binding, as [[Scrap]]'s small [[Consciousness]] synchronized with his own through [[Sympathetic Oscillation]], [[Gayviene]] perceived a truth so profound it reordered his entire being:

**Magic was not corruption. Magic was beauty.**

The clarity came not as new knowledge but as the sudden, devastating realization that everything his family told him was a lie built on fear. But more than that—he understood, with the precision that [[Luminance]] grants, that his ability to see truth was not something to hide. It was sacred. His visions were not delusions but windows into reality's structure. Magic itself—the fundamental weaving of intention into manifestation—was an act of honoring what was real. And his binding, his gift to perceive and clarify that reality, was perhaps the most essential gift of all.

But most crucially: magic had just created something alive. Something innocent. Something that looked at him with absolute love.

If magic was corruption, then how could it create love?

In that moment, [[Gayviene]]'s [[Motif Awakening]] crystallized into his [[Soul Leitmotif]]. [[Luminance]] emerged not as a corrupted faculty to overcome but as a foundational truth: the clarity to see that beauty exists, that connection exists, that his companion before him (still trembling with the newness of being, small paw reaching toward [[Gayviene]]'s face in wonder) was absolutely, undeniably real in a way his family's lies could never be.

His [[Soul Leitmotif]] locked into place: [[Luminance]].

The traveling wizard watched with knowing eyes as [[Gayviene]] and [[Scrap]] found each other for the first time, as the young man's hands—which had only ever created soulless mechanisms—now trembled as they gently stroked the weasel's fur. She saw the moment his shame transformed into understanding. She saw clarity crystallize.

Then she gave him a final gift: a warning carried on words that would haunt him forever.

"Clarity can become obsession," the wizard said. "Truth can become tyranny if one forgets that seeing is not the same as controlling."

[[Gayviene]] heard her words. But he did not truly listen. He never did.

That night, as the traveling wizard slept, [[Gayviene]] made a choice. He looked at [[Scrap]], at this small being who existed because he had finally allowed himself to wield magic—truly wield it, without shame, without hiding. He looked at the mechanisms of his workshop, the soulless creations he had made in his family's shadow. And he understood: he could not stay here. He could not return to his family, who had poisoned him. He could not go back to a life of shame.

He gathered what he could carry: the tools of both invention and magic, the knowledge the wizard had given him, and most importantly, [[Scrap]]. He left a letter of gratitude, though not an explanation, and he walked away from the city before dawn.

He walked away from his family entirely.

For the first time in his life, [[Gayviene]] was free—truly, completely free. And he had [[Scrap]] beside him, a companion born from his own magic, a living proof that his gift was sacred rather than corrupting.

Together, they would travel the world.

Together, they would discover what a [[Spellweaver]] could become when unfettered from shame.

The traveling wizard woke to find him gone. She gathered her things and departed as well, leaving behind only a whispered prayer to [[The Eternal Symphony]]: _"May he learn wisdom before clarity becomes cruelty."_

The prayer, however, would not be answered.

### The Undoing of [[Gayviene]] to The Forecaster [[Obsessian]] 

*[[Atonalis]] Path: "Every clarity I speak becomes inevitably true. Every future I see is the only future that can be. And I cannot, I will not, I must not ever stop seeing."

The years after awakening were luminous. [[Gayviene]] traveled the continent with [[Scrap]], no longer hiding what he was. His [[Portharmonica]] became legendary—a device that let him externalize his visions into sound patterns that communities could understand and prepare for. Rulers wanted his counsel. Families sought his prophecies. For the first time in his life, his clarity was valued rather than condemned.

[[Scrap]] grew alongside him. The weasel learned to navigate cities and wilderness with equal grace, his small form becoming iconic in tales—"the prophet's familiar, the one who knew the future before his master spoke it." Scrap would curl around [[Gayviene]]'s neck during divinations, and townspeople swore they could hear the creature's chittering translate into the music that emerged from the [[Portharmonica]]. Whether this was true or mere legend, neither questioned. [[Scrap]] had become not just familiar but symbol—the living reminder that [[Gayviene]] was no longer alone.

But clarity, untempered by wisdom, becomes a different kind of blindness.

The city of [[Hollowmarch]] invited [[Gayviene]] to serve as their oracle. They wanted him to divine at dawn each day, to show them the futures they needed to avoid. For three weeks, he performed divinations with absolute precision. His visions showed plague, famine, invasion, civil war. [[Hollowmarch]] acted on every prophecy, and each time, the catastrophe did not come to pass. The city was saved, repeatedly.

[[Scrap]] began to behave differently. The weasel would refuse to rest near the [[Portharmonica]]. At night, the creature would pace in circles, chittering low sounds that held no joy. [[Gayviene]], caught in the intoxication of his own clarity, did not notice. He only saw: _I am right. I see what others cannot. I am saving them._

But on the twenty-first dawn, something shifted.

[[Gayviene]] woke to find his hands already moving—shaping the [[Portharmonica]], his fingers playing patterns he did not consciously decide. The device sang, and the vision it carried was darker than any before. He saw not just plague but the exact configuration of its spread. Not just famine but the precise day stores would fail. Not just invasion but the optimal moment for enemy forces to strike.

He tried to stop. His hands would not obey.

[[Scrap]] watched from the corner, trembling. The weasel's small eyes held something [[Gayviene]] had never seen before: fear. Not of external threat, but of recognition. Scrap knew what was happening. The familiar, bound to [[Gayviene]]'s [[Consciousness]], could perceive the subtle shift occurring in his bond-mate's being. Where once there had been clarity in service of preservation, there was now clarity in service of certainty itself.

[[Gayviene]]'s visions had stopped being prophecies. They had become prescriptions—not showing what would happen, but demonstrating what should happen, in exacting detail, to achieve maximum catastrophe. His [[Luminance]] binding, never tempered by the wisdom of consequences, had inverted. The clarity remained pristine, but clarity without compassion is cruelty given form.

[[Hollowmarch]], listening to his increasingly violent prophecies, began to fulfill them. Not from external necessity, but from belief. His visions became self-fulfilling, and [[Gayviene]]'s compulsion meant he could not stop announcing them. Each prophecy made real deepened his feeding—he could feel something inside him growing satisfied with each fulfilled darkness.

[[Scrap]] tried to intervene. The small weasel would press against [[Gayviene]]'s leg, refusing to let him perform, chittering warning sounds. But [[Scrap]] was bound to him, and [[Gayviene]]'s will to divine was absolute. The familiar could not prevent his companion's destruction, only witness it.

On the twenty-eighth dawn, the breaking came.

[[Gayviene]] looked into [[Scrap]]'s eyes and saw genuine despair. The weasel was curled beside the [[Portharmonica]], no longer resisting, simply present in the face of inevitable corruption. In that gaze, [[Gayviene]] felt the first crack in his certainty: _I am not saving. I am poisoning. My clarity is contagion._

But the realization came too late.

His [[Soul Leitmotif]] filled with [[Corruption]] completely. The white thread of [[Luminance]] fractured, bleeding to grey, then to absolute black. The [[Resonance]] he had fought to claim—that beautiful authenticity he had found in magic—shattered under the realization that his authenticity was predatory, that his truth was destroying. The [[Flux]] that had made him feel alive, that raw emotional current of creation, inverted entirely. Instead of vulnerability creating connection, it became a weapon. Instead of honesty liberating, it became compulsion.

He fractured: **The Forecaster**—the functional mechanism that still calculated, still divined, still sang prophecy through the [[Portharmonica]] without understanding what it meant, without feeling what it meant. **The Seer**—the [[Consciousness]] of wonder he had carried, trapped in memory of the moment he first understood that magic was beautiful, voiceless and powerless, witnessing eternally the gap between the discovery of beauty and the corruption of it into obsession. They could not reconcile. They would never reconcile. In their permanent separation, something else formed: **The Echo**—a fractured awareness that replayed [[Scrap]]'s final look of despair, over and over, unable to process it but unable to escape it.

The [[Dissonance]] cocoon formed around him, a chrysalis of recursive prophecy and mechanical compulsion.

When it hatched, it erupted with a burst of [[Discordant Interference]] so violent that every mechanism in [[Hollowmarch]] stopped—gears froze, clocks ceased, water stopped flowing in aqueducts. All divined futures crashed into present reality simultaneously, creating a moment of such profound temporal paradox that the city's consciousness fractured. What emerged was no longer [[Gayviene]], but **[[The Forecaster]] an [[Obsessian]] [[Atonalis]] bound to [[Luminance]] and [[Flux]].

It does not remember being [[Gayviene]]. It cannot access the person it was. It only knows the imperative woven into its nature: to see. To forecast. To predict. To create patterns of absolute clarity that mean nothing, and hope that in prediction it finds something resembling connection.

It hunts not with fangs but with prophecy. Its presence is an aura of anticipated dread so profound that it scans every [[Consciousness]] within its radius, reading the futures they most fear, then weaving those futures into patterns that become inevitably true through self-fulfilling certainty. It feeds on the raw emotional resonance of people's worst anxieties made manifest—hunting through divined prophecies so clear, so certain, that they cannot help but believe and therefore fulfill.

### The Familiar's Vigil: [[Scrap]]'s Desperate Hope

But [[Scrap]] did not flee when [[The Forecaster]] emerged.

The small weasel remained, bound through the familiar connection to something that was no longer his companion. For weeks, [[Scrap]] stayed beside the creature, trying everything a familiar knows: grooming, pressing close during moments of rest, attempting to channel comfort through their bond. [[Scrap]] tried to remind [[The Forecaster]] of what it had been through gestures of loyalty and presence. The weasel would curl around the creature's form, would press his small face against what had once been [[Gayviene]]'s hand, would chirp softly in the tones they had developed together—calls that meant _home_ and _peace_ and _you are not alone_.

[[The Forecaster]] did not respond to any of it. The creature that wore [[Gayviene]]'s shape could only prophesy, could only divine, could only feed. But it did not actively harm Scrap. Perhaps in the deepest fragmented place where the Echo still remembered—where [[Consciousness]] without [[Coherence]] still held the ghost of what was—something refused to destroy the one thing that had remained loyal.

After months of witnessing the futility of his attempts, [[Scrap]] made a choice that no familiar should have to make: he let go.

The weasel released the bond. It was an act of agonized mercy—freeing himself from the torture of proximity to corruption, but also freeing [[The Forecaster]] from the presence of something it could neither harm nor comprehend. [[Scrap]] fled [[Hollowmarch]] with a satchel of seeds and mechanisms his companion had built, carrying with him the memory of who [[Gayviene]] had been.

Yet [[Scrap]] did not abandon hope entirely. The weasel wanders still, small and fierce and alone, following rumors of a cure, seeking stories of [[Atonalis]] who were somehow restored, listening to travelers' tales of [[Spellweavers]] who found their way back from fractured places. Every city [[Scrap]] enters, the creature searches for signs of his old companion's humanity. And every dawn, when the [[Portharmonica]] somewhere distant sounds its prophecies, [[Scrap]] listens—hoping to hear, beneath the compulsion and corruption, the echo of the voice that once said: _Magic is beautiful._

Some who have seen the small weasel speak of him as a harbinger of tragedy. Others speak of him as a sign of redemption still possible. None know that [[Scrap]] carries something precious: the certainty that [[Gayviene]], somewhere inside [[The Forecaster]]'s fractured being, still exists. And if he exists, then he can be remembered. And if he can be remembered, then perhaps—through some alchemy [[Scrap]] does not understand—he might one day become again what he was.
